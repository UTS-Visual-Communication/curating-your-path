Uploading your website to the server.

Ensure you know where your files are on your computer. We will be uploading all of your website, so it will look something like the image to the right

You will need an FTP (File Transfer Protocol) program to connect to the www.educ.dab.uts.edu.au server. FTP is the standard transfer protocol used to transfer files over the internet, you can use FTP to download files from a server as well as upload files to a server. We use Cyberduck. If you have not already done so, download and install it from the link above

Below are the instructions on how to connect.

  1. open cyberduck
  2. click on Open Connection
  3. for server copy and paste in the following adress ftp://www.educ.dab.uts.edu.au/web
  4. For the username and password use your student number and usual UTS password
  5. Ensure that the dropdown at the top of the dialogue now reads:
    FTP (File Transfer protocol)
  6. Then hit connect
  7. This may take a moment or two, but it will open up a window with a lot of folders in it, look for the one that is your student number and double click it.
  8. Once it is open, drag all of your webfiles in to upload them. not the main folder, but the content of the folder
  9. Go to your URL http://www.educ.dab.uts.edu.au/web/123456 (replace 123456 with your student number)

Remember that when we go to any directory on the internet the first file that is served up by default is index.html, therefore the file that will be sent to your browser via http will be the index.html file you just copied onto the sites server. You can then follow the links on this page to your other pages of your folio
